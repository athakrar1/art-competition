C

Caption

Dancing Pokemon: In the world of Pokemon, three collage students are giving their first hip-hop show! Lets support them!

How is the artwork created?

As huge Pokemon fans, we created a short animation named: Dancing Pokemon, where we animated three pokemons (Lucario and two dragonites) and taught them how to hip hop. We implemented the animation in blender. In terms of the procedures, we first downloaded coarse 3D static meshes (models) from Free 3D (https://free3d.com/3d-models/pokemon) and subdivided them to smooth the mesh edges. Then, we downloaded the animation fbx files from maximo.com (Highly recommended) and did the rigging and parent the animations with the models. Finally, we adjusted the light condition as well as the surface material to make the rendered animation more realistic. We rendered the video using blender and converted it to gif using ffmpeg. Hope you like it! Pokemmmmmon Go!!

How long did it take?

5 hours learning + 2 hours creating

E

Caption

This is a model of sad Oski crying to reflect our current emotional states.

How is the artwork created?

We created this model using Blender by using tools such as loop cutting or extruding various edges/faces on the initial default cube. To ensure that certain shapes were sharp enough to distinguish themselves from the model's base (i.e. the ears and the nose), we subdivided the faces of these two areas. After passing this through our project 2 code, we used loop division to make the surface look smoother, and as we pre-subdivided the faces on the ears and nose, they were sharper and more distinguishable rather than being overly smooth. Finally, as post-processing, we took the screenshot of the model, imported into Photoshop, and applied color on top of the model via layers of the blending modes "Color" and "Darken" to demonstrate that this was Oski and that the objects extruding from his eyes were tears.

How long did it take?

80 minutes
